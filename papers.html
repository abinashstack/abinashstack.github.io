1. LLM Foundations

Attention Is All You Need (2017)

https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762

Imagine reading a book but instead of one word at a time, you see the entire page at once. For every word, you highlight other words most important for understanding it. The Transformer does this — looks at ALL words simultaneously and figures out which are most related. Before this, AI read through a tiny keyhole, one word at a time.

  OLD WAY (through a keyhole):
     "The" --> "cat" --> "sat" --> "on" --> "the" --> "mat"
      Each word waits for the one before. Slow!

  TRANSFORMER WAY (whole page at once):
      "The"    "cat"    "sat"    "on"    "the"    "mat"
        |  \   / |  \   / |  \   / |  \   / |  \   / |
        |   \ /  |   \ /  |   \ /  |   \ /  |   \ /  |
        |    X   |    X   |    X   |    X   |    X   |
        |   / \  |   / \  |   / \  |   / \  |   / \  |
        v  /   \ v  /   \ v  /   \ v  /   \ v  /   \ v
      [Every word checks every other word. All at once!]

Why it matters: This single invention is the engine inside virtually every modern AI system.

BERT (2018)

https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.04805

Fill-in-the-blank quiz: 'The ___ chased the mouse.' BERT reads in BOTH directions — it sees words before AND after the blank. Previous AI could only see what came before. Once it got good at fill-in-the-blank, it could be taught specific jobs like spam detection.

  OLD WAY: Only reads left-to-right
      "The"  "cat"  "___"  "the"  "mouse"
       -->    -->    ???    ||||   ||||||    (can't see!)

  BERT: Reads BOTH directions
       ------>  ------>  ------>  ------>
      "The"    "cat"    "[MASK]"  "the"   "mouse"
       <------  <------  <------  <------
      BERT guesses: "chased"  Correct!

Why it matters: One AI brain could learn language deeply, then be quickly adapted to dozens of jobs.

GPT-2 (2019)

A child who does nothing but predict the next word in conversations for years. Nobody teaches grammar. Over time, it can write essays, summarize stories, translate — without being taught. Lesson: make the model bigger + more text = new abilities just appear.

  TRAINING: Just predict the next word, millions of times
      "Once upon a ___"              --> "time"
      "The capital of France is ___" --> "Paris"
      x 1,500,000,000 brain cells

  RESULT: Abilities nobody taught it!
      [Write stories] [Summarize articles] [Translate]
      All from just predicting the next word!

Why it matters: Scale up a simple recipe and surprising new abilities emerge on their own.

GPT-3 (2020)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165

175 billion brain cells. Discovery: 'in-context learning.' Show it a few examples in the prompt and it figures out new tasks without retraining. Anyone could program AI just by talking to it.

  PROMPT:
  English: "hello"    -> French: "bonjour"    (example 1)
  English: "goodbye"  -> French: "au revoir"  (example 2)
  English: "thank you"-> French: ???

  GPT-3: "merci"   No retraining needed!

Why it matters: Teach AI new tasks just by giving examples in plain English.

Scaling Laws (2020)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361

Found precise mathematical formulas predicting model performance based on size, data, and compute. Smooth, predictable curves. Turned AI from guesswork into engineering.

  +----------+    +----------+    +----------+
  | MODEL    |    | TRAINING |    | COMPUTE  |
  | SIZE     | +  | DATA     | +  | (GPUs)   | = Predictable Quality
  +----------+    +----------+    +----------+
  [ Precise formula tells you exactly what to expect! ]

Why it matters: Reliable roadmap — 'How big?' became solvable math.

InstructGPT (2022)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.02155

Like dog training. Humans show good examples, rank outputs, build a scorecard (reward model). AI practices using the scorecard. This process (RLHF) is the secret sauce that turned raw GPT into ChatGPT.

  Step 1: Humans show good examples
  Step 2: Humans rank outputs -> build Reward Model
  Step 3: Model improves using scorecard
       +--------+  generate   +-----------+  score  +----------+
       | GPT-3  | ---------> | REWARD    | ------> | Update   |
       |        | <--------- | MODEL     |         | & repeat |
       +--------+  improve   +-----------+         +----------+
  BEFORE RLHF: "Here is a 2000-word essay about dolphins..."
  AFTER  RLHF: "Sure! Here are 3 key facts about dolphins."

Why it matters: Cracked making powerful AI obedient and helpful — direct ancestor of ChatGPT.

LLaMA (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.13971

Smaller model trained on MORE data beats model 10x its size. LLaMA-13B beat GPT-3-175B. Like a small kitchen that practices recipes a thousand times beating a giant one. Made weights open for everyone.

  GPT-3:  175B params x 300B tokens = "big oven, few recipes"
  LLaMA:   13B params x  1T+ tokens = "small oven, TONS of practice"

  Result:  LLaMA 13B >= GPT-3 175B on many benchmarks!
  AND it's open source!

Why it matters: Don't need a billion-dollar budget for top-tier AI. Open access.

Llama 2 (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.09288

Improved second edition: 40% more data, longer memory, RLHF chat versions, published safety testing. Like giving away the car free WITH seatbelts, airbags, and crash-test report.

  LLaMA v1 --> Llama 2:
  + 40% more training data
  + Longer memory (4K context)
  + RLHF chat versions
  + Safety testing & report
  = Free car WITH seatbelts and airbags

Why it matters: Gold standard for releasing powerful open AI responsibly.

Mixtral 8x7B (2024)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.04088

Hospital with 8 specialist doctors. Triage nurse sends each patient to just 2 of 8. Total 47B params, only 13B active at a time. Matches 70B models at fraction of cost.

  Word comes in -> ROUTER picks 2 of 8 experts
                      /                     \
                  [Expert 3]            [Expert 7]
                      \                     /
                       -> Combined answer

  TOTAL: 47B  |  ACTIVE: 13B  |  MATCHES: ~70B models

Why it matters: Smart routing of specialists is cheaper, faster, and just as good.

DeepSeek-V2 (2024)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.04434

Compressed conversation memory (KV cache) by 90% using shorthand that expands on demand. Plus 160 tiny specialist experts (vs Mixtral's 8 big ones). Way cheaper to run.

  Normal model:   Full notes --> HUGE memory (100%)
  DeepSeek-V2:    Shorthand  --> 10% memory, expand on demand

  Also: 160 fine-grained experts (pick 6) vs 8 big experts (pick 2)
  Same quality, dramatically cheaper to serve

Why it matters: Slashed memory costs by ~93%, making powerful AI affordable at scale.

DeepSeek-R1 (2025)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.12948

No textbook, no tutor — just 'right/wrong' feedback. Through pure RL, the model spontaneously started checking its own work. Nobody programmed it. Matches OpenAI's o1. Open source.

  Traditional:  Human writes "Step 1... Step 2..." -> AI copies
  DeepSeek-R1:  AI tries -> Right/Wrong -> repeat millions of times

  EMERGED ON ITS OWN:
  "The answer is 42... Wait, let me verify...
   Step 3 seems wrong... Let me redo it...
   Actually, the answer is 47."

Why it matters: AI can learn to reason and self-correct through practice alone.

2. Reasoning & Agents

Chain-of-Thought Prompting (2022)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.11903

Show a kid how to solve a math problem by writing out each step, they copy the pattern. Same with AI — show it examples with thinking written out, and it stops skipping steps.

  WITHOUT CoT:  [Question] -------> [Answer] (often wrong!)

  WITH CoT:     [Question]
                    |
                [Step 1: First, I notice...]
                    |
                [Step 2: That means...]
                    |
                [Step 3: So I calculate...]
                    |
                [Answer] (much better!)

Why it matters: Simply asking AI to 'show its work' dramatically improves accuracy.

Self-Consistency (2022)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.11171

Ask AI the same question multiple times with different reasoning paths, pick the answer that comes up most often. Like asking 10 friends independently and going with the majority.

                    [Same Question]
                   /    |    |    \
                  v     v    v     v
              [Path A][Path B][Path C][Path D]
                 |      |      |       |
                (42)   (42)   (37)    (42)
                  \     |      |      /
                  VOTE: 42 wins! (3/4 agree)

Why it matters: Majority vote catches mistakes a single attempt would miss.

Tree of Thoughts (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.10601

Chess player thinking: explore multiple paths, evaluate each, backtrack from dead ends. Instead of one chain, AI branches like a tree.

                     [Problem]
                    /    |    \
                [Idea A][Idea B][Idea C]
                /    \     |       |
            [A1]   [A2]  [B1]   [C1]
            Good!   XX   Good!   XX      XX = dead end
             |             |
           *BEST*       (backup)

Why it matters: Explore, evaluate, and backtrack lets AI solve creative puzzles.

Scaling Test-Time Compute (2024)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.03314

Two ways to improve: (1) bigger brain (expensive), (2) more thinking time (cheaper). A smaller AI that thinks longer can beat a larger model that answers quickly.

  OLD: Bigger brain -> $$$$$$$$$, Quick answer (decent)
  NEW: Smaller brain -> $$$, Think...think...check... -> Answer!

  Cost: LESS     Result: Often BETTER!

Why it matters: Better answers by spending compute at question time, not just training.

ReAct (2022)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.03629

Detective solving a mystery: think ('victim was at docks'), act (go to docks), observe (find receipt), think again. AI alternates between reasoning and taking real actions.

  THOUGHT --> ACTION --> OBSERVE
  "I need     [Search]    "Result:
   to find"   "Einstein    Born 1879"
               birth"
       ^                      |
       +----------------------+
       v
  THOUGHT --> ACTION --> OBSERVE --> FINAL ANSWER

Why it matters: Combining thinking with real actions grounds AI in facts.

Toolformer (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.04761

Student discovers calculators, search engines on their own through trial and error. AI taught itself which tools help and when — no rules programmed.

  BEFORE: "What is 3847 x 192?"  -> [AI Brain] -> "738,624?" (WRONG)

  AFTER:  "What is 3847 x 192?"
          [AI Brain]: "I should use a calculator"
          [Calculator] -> 738,624   <- AI LEARNS this helps!
          "The answer is 738,624" (CORRECT)

Why it matters: AI figures out tool use on its own.

Voyager (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.16291

Minecraft player with a recipe book. Learns skills, saves them, builds on them forever. Uses old skills to learn harder ones.

  EXPLORE -> SET GOAL -> TRY IT -> Worked? Save to SKILL LIBRARY
                                       |
                                   Failed? Try again with fixes
  SKILL LIBRARY: [chop_tree] [build_shelter] [mine_iron] ...growing!

Why it matters: AI that builds a growing library of reusable skills — endless learning.

Generative Agents (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.03442

25 AI characters in a virtual town, each with memories. They plan their day, form relationships, organize events — all emergently. Like The Sims powered by ChatGPT.

  6AM  [Wake up] -> MEMORY STREAM (diary of everything)
           |
       [REFLECT] "I've been spending time with Sam... friends."
           |
       [PLAN] 1. Finish painting 2. Meet Sam 3. Go to party
           |
       [Live the day, make new memories]
           |
  10PM [Sleep] ...memories gently fade...

Why it matters: Give AI memory + reflection + planning = believable society emerges.

AutoGen (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.08155

Team building a house: specialists pass messages. 'Here's my code.' 'Bug on line 12.' 'Fixed. Test again?' AutoGen builds AI teams. A human can jump in anytime.

  +-----------+     +-----------+     +-----------+
  |  WRITER   |---->| REVIEWER  |---->|  TESTER   |
  | "Here's   |     | "Bug on   |     | "All tests|
  |  my code" |     |  line 12" |     |  pass now"|
  +-----------+     +-----------+     +-----------+
  HUMAN (you) can jump in anytime!

Why it matters: Assemble specialized AI agents that collaborate like coworkers.

LLM Autonomous Agents Survey (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.11432

Encyclopedia of every AI agent design: brain (LLM), memory (experiences), hands (tools). The field guide to the whole landscape.

  THE ANATOMY OF AN AI AGENT:
       +---------------------------+
       |       BRAIN (LLM)         |
       +---+-----------+-----------+
           |           |           |
      [PLANNING]  [MEMORY]    [ACTION]
      Break tasks  Short/Long  Use tools
      into steps   term recall  Search, code

Why it matters: The single best map of the AI agents territory.

SWE-agent (2024)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.15793

Brilliant plumber with no map vs. one with a floor plan. Interface design matters as much as intelligence. Custom workspace -> AI fixed 12.5% of real GitHub bugs.

  BAD: Raw terminal -> AI lost -> 0% fixed
  GOOD: Custom interface:
    > search("bug")
    > open(file.py)
    > edit(line 42)
    > run_tests()
  Result: 12.5% of real bugs fixed!

Why it matters: Workspace design matters as much as how smart the AI is.

3. Multimodal & Vision

CLIP (2021)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.00020

Child who grew up with 400M picture books. Matches images with text descriptions. Can recognize things nobody trained it on, just by reading a text description.

  [Photo of dog]        "A golden retriever in a park"
       |                          |
   IMAGE ENCODER           TEXT ENCODER
       |                          |
   [vector] <-- MATCH? --> [vector]
   If match --> "Yes, this IS a dog in a park!"

Why it matters: Understand images through language — recognize anything you can describe.

Flamingo (2022)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.14198

Show it 3-4 examples of 'image + question + answer' and it figures out new images. Like a quick learner who picks up tasks by watching someone a few times.

  Example 1: [cat]   "Animal?" -> "Yes"
  Example 2: [truck] "Animal?" -> "No"
  Example 3: [rose]  "Animal?" -> "No"
  NEW:        [horse] "Animal?" -> "Yes!" (after just 3 examples!)

Why it matters: Learn new visual tasks from just a handful of examples.

LLaVA (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.08485

Connected 'seeing' AI (CLIP) to 'talking' AI (LLaMA) with a simple bridge. Like hiring a translator between a blind genius and a mute observer. Open source.

  [Birthday party photo]
        |
  CLIP (the eye) -> "cake, kids, balloons"
        |
  BRIDGE (translator)
        |
  LLaMA (the brain) -> "A child's birthday party!"

Why it matters: Build see-and-talk AI by connecting existing parts with a thin bridge — for free.

GPT-4V (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.08774

The smartest person you know suddenly gets perfect eyesight. Already wrote essays and passed the bar exam — now can also understand photos, charts, memes, handwriting.

  BEFORE: "Tell me about sunsets"  -> Generic text answer
  AFTER:  [Photo of sunset] "Tell me about this"
          -> "This is a sunset over the ocean. The orange
              and pink hues suggest late summer, taken
              from a west-facing beach..."

Why it matters: First AI product millions could use to discuss anything they photograph.

Segment Anything / SAM (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.02643

Magic scissors for images. Point at any object in any photo and it perfectly outlines it. Trained on 1 billion masks. Works on anything.

  ORIGINAL PHOTO:  [clouds] [sun] [house] [tree]
                                         ^
                                    YOU POINT HERE
                                         |
                                    SAM outlines:
                                    +----------+
                                    | *HOUSE!* |
                                    +----------+
  Works on ANY object in ANY image

Why it matters: Instantly cut out any object from any photo.

DDPM (2020)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.11239

Start with painting, gradually add noise until pure static. Train AI to reverse: turn static back into image step by step. Foundation of ALL modern image generators.

  FORWARD:  [Clean photo] -> [Fuzzy] -> [Noisy] -> [Static]
  REVERSE (AI learns this):
            [Static] -> [Vaguely something] -> [Almost] -> [Clean!]
  Step by step, static becomes a picture!

Why it matters: The engine inside every major AI image generator.

Latent Diffusion / Stable Diffusion (2021)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.10752

Compress image to tiny version, do creative work there, decompress back. Same quality, way faster. Made AI art accessible on regular laptops.

  OLD: [Static 1024x1024] --slow--> [Image 1024x1024] EXPENSIVE
  NEW: [Compress to 64x64] --fast--> [Decompress to 1024x1024]
       Same quality. Runs on YOUR computer.

Why it matters: AI art generation fast and cheap enough for regular laptops.

DALL-E 2 (2022)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.06125

Type 'a teddy bear skateboarding in Times Square' -> CLIP understands concept -> diffusion paints it. Brought AI art to mainstream public.

  "An astronaut riding a horse on the moon"
        |
  [CLIP brain] -> [concept embedding] -> [Diffusion painter]
        |
  A brand-new image that never existed before!

Why it matters: AI-generated art went mainstream.

Imagen (2022)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.11487

Making TEXT understanding bigger matters more than making IMAGE part bigger. A well-read artist who understands your words beats a technically skilled one who barely speaks your language.

  Others: Small text brain + GIANT painter -> "Close, not quite"
  Imagen: GIANT text brain + Normal painter -> "Exactly right!"
  WINNER: Better listening beats better painting!

Why it matters: Key to better AI art is better listening, not better painting.

Stable Diffusion 3 / MMDiT (2024)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.03206

Text and image flow through SAME network, constantly influencing each other. Like architect and builder standing shoulder to shoulder. Finally solved text-in-images.

  OLD: Text sent occasionally to painter (works alone)
  NEW (MMDiT):
    Layer 1: [text] <==TALK==> [image]
    Layer 2: [text] <==TALK==> [image]
    Layer N: [text] <==TALK==> [image] -> Final image
  Text and image talk at EVERY step!

Why it matters: Solved the problem of AI art being terrible at text in images.

InternVL (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.14238

Most systems invested in bigger brains but kept 'eyes' small. InternVL scaled vision to 6B params. Great eyes matter as much as great brain.

  Before: small vision (300M) + big brain -> "I think a red car?"
  After:  HUGE vision (6B)  + big brain -> "Red 2019 Tesla Model 3,
                                             dented bumper."

Why it matters: AI's 'eyes' as important as its 'brain.'

Sora (2024)

https://openai.com/index/video-generation-models-as-world-simulators/

Generates 1-minute videos from text. Understands physics, 3D, camera movement. Treats video as spacetime patches, fills them in coherently.

  "A drone shot over a coral reef"
        |
  Video = grid of SPACETIME PATCHES
  +-----+-----+-----+-----+-----+
  |t=0  |t=1  |t=2  |t=3  |t=4  |
  +-----+-----+-----+-----+-----+
  Diffusion Transformer fills each -> smooth 1-min video

Why it matters: Leap from still images to coherent, physics-aware video.

4. RLHF, Alignment & Fine-tuning

Fine-Tuning LMs from Human Preferences (2019)

https://arxiv.org/abs/1909.08593

Like training a dog: try, get feedback, build auto-judge, practice. The original recipe behind ChatGPT's training methodology.

  AI tries -> Human says "good/bad" -> Build REWARD MODEL
  -> AI practices against reward model -> Gets better
  The original recipe behind ChatGPT

Why it matters: Foundational recipe for teaching AI what humans want.

LoRA (2021)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.09685

Freeze the grand piano, clip on a tiny attachment. 10,000x fewer trainable params, same performance. Like adding a capo to a guitar instead of rebuilding it.

  Traditional: Change ALL billions of settings -> $$$, days
  LoRA:        Freeze + tiny adapters          -> $, hours
  Parameters:  10,000x fewer
  Result:      Both equally good!

Why it matters: Affordable AI customization for anyone.

Prefix-Tuning (2021)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2101.00190

Sticky notes on a textbook. 0.1% of parameters updated, steers the whole model. The textbook never changes.

  Layer 1: [Sticky Note] --> | Frozen Chapter 1 |
  Layer 2: [Sticky Note] --> | Frozen Chapter 2 |
  Layer N: [Sticky Note] --> | Frozen Chapter N |
  Only sticky notes are trainable! 0.1% of parameters.

Why it matters: Steer a giant AI with absurdly small changes.

Constitutional AI (2022)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.08073

Give AI a list of rules ('constitution'), let it self-check and revise before anyone sees it. Automates safety without humans reviewing every response.

  THE CONSTITUTION: 1. Be helpful 2. Be honest 3. Be harmless
  AI writes -> Checks own rules -> "Does this break any rules?"
  NO -> Send it!  |  YES -> Revise, check again

Why it matters: AI learns safety at scale without human reviewing every response.

Reward Model Overoptimization (2022)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.10760

Student games the test: score goes up, knowledge goes down. Same with AI — optimizing too hard against reward makes true quality decrease. Goodhart's Law.

  TRUE quality:   peaks then FALLS with more optimization
  REWARD score:   keeps rising (but it's being GAMED)
  THE GAP = Overoptimization (Goodhart's Law)

Why it matters: Blindly maximizing AI scores can make it worse, not better.

DPO (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.18290

Old way: 3 complex steps (rank, reward model, RL). DPO: show 'good vs bad' pairs + simple formula. No reward model, no RL needed.

  OLD (RLHF): 3 steps, 4+ models, HIGH complexity -> Good
  NEW (DPO):  1 step,  1 model,  LOW complexity  -> Equally good!

Why it matters: Preference training accessible to everyone.

QLoRA (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.14314

Compress model to 4-bit + attach LoRA adapters. Fine-tune a 65B model on a SINGLE consumer GPU. Like compressing a 4K movie for your phone.

  Full: 65B params, 16-bit -> ~130 GB -> server room
  QLoRA: 65B params, 4-bit + LoRA -> ~48 GB -> 1 consumer GPU!
  Quality: 99.3% of ChatGPT

Why it matters: Powerful AI customization on hobbyist hardware.

Representation Engineering (2023)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.01405

Found 'volume knobs' inside AI for honesty, morality, power-seeking. Can read and adjust them at runtime. Like a mixing board in a recording studio.

  THE AI's INTERNAL MIXING BOARD:
    Honesty     Morality    Power-seeking
   [====*===]  [===*====]  [===*====]
     turn UP     turn UP     turn DOWN
  Read & adjust these "personality dials" in real time!

Why it matters: Control AI behavior through internal 'personality dials.'

SPIN (2024)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.01335

Painter compares work to masterpiece, improves each round. Self-competition without human feedback — each version of the AI tries to beat its old self.

  Round 1: Human responses vs AI v0 -> AI v1 (better!)
  Round 2: Human responses vs AI v1 -> AI v2 (even better!)
  Round 3: ...each round harder, each version closer to human

Why it matters: AI self-improves through self-competition.

KTO (2024)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.01306

Only needs thumbs up/down on individual responses (not pairs). Based on psychology: humans feel losses ~2x as strongly as gains. Much cheaper data.

  OLD (DPO): "Which is better, A or B?" (expensive pairs)
  NEW (KTO): "Was this good or bad?" (simple, cheap)
             Thumbs down weighs 2x more (loss aversion)

Why it matters: Much cheaper data collection for alignment.

ORPO (2024)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.07691

Merge 'teach language' and 'teach preferences' into ONE training run. Like a school teaching reading and manners in the same class. No reference model needed.

  OLD: Step 1 (language) THEN Step 2 (preferences)
  ORPO: Both AT THE SAME TIME in one step. Done!
  Half the time, half the compute.

Why it matters: Cuts training pipeline nearly in half.

5. RAG, Embeddings & Efficient Inference

REALM (2020)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2002.08909

Student + librarian learn together. Every time the student studies, the librarian finds the best page. Both improve together over time.

  LIBRARIAN (Retriever) <--> STUDENT (Language Model)
       |                              |
  finds best page              reads and learns
  from encyclopedia            from that page
  Both improve with every question!

Why it matters: AI that looks things up while learning = smarter, more factual.

RAG (2020)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.11401

Open-book exam. When you ask a question, AI first searches a textbook for relevant pages, then writes its answer using those pages. Like a friend who checks notes instead of guessing.

  Question -> SEARCH ENGINE finds relevant docs
                         |
                    [Doc 1] [Doc 2] [Doc 3]
                         \     |     /
                    SMART WRITER reads docs
                         |
                    Grounded answer

Why it matters: Answers backed by sources — reduces 'making things up.'

Dense Passage Retrieval (2020)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.04906

Every passage gets a 'meaning fingerprint.' Find the closest match by meaning, not exact words. Like finding friends by shared interests, not matching name tags.

  OLD: Search words "dog learning tricks"
       -> MISS: "Canine Training Techniques" (different words!)
  NEW (DPR): Compare meaning fingerprints
       -> MATCH! (similar meaning, different words)

Why it matters: Search by meaning, not keywords — dramatically more accurate.

Sentence-BERT (2019)

https://arxiv.org/abs/1908.10084

Comparing 10,000 sentences: 65 hours before, 5 seconds after. Each sentence gets a permanent 'vibe code' in one pass. Comparing is just comparing two number lists.

  BEFORE: Feed BOTH sentences in for each pair -> 65 HOURS
  AFTER:  Stamp each sentence once -> Compare stamps -> 5 SECONDS
  "I love cats" -> [0.9, 0.2, 0.1, ...]
  "I adore kittens" -> [0.8, 0.3, 0.1, ...]  SIMILAR!

Why it matters: Compare millions of sentences instantly.

Matryoshka Representation Learning (2022)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.13147

Russian nesting dolls: first 64 dims are decent, 128 better, 256 great. One model, many sizes. Use short for speed, long for accuracy.

  [64 dims ] = Quick & decent
  [128 dims     ] = Better
  [256 dims            ] = Great
  [512 dims                   ] = Excellent
  [1024 dims                           ] = Best
  Each smaller doll works on its own!

Why it matters: One model at different quality/speed levels.

GPTQ (2022)

Speculative Decoding (2022)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.17192

Fast intern drafts 5-8 words, boss checks all at once. Most accepted. 2-3x faster, mathematically identical output. A rare 'free lunch.'

  WITHOUT: Boss writes one word at a time  -> 4 seconds
  WITH:    Intern drafts 4 words (0.2s)
           Boss checks all at once (1s)    -> 1.2 seconds!
           Same exact output! 2-3x faster!

Why it matters: 2-3x faster with zero quality loss — a rare 'free lunch.'

  P(output) = P(big model alone) -- GUARANTEED
