                        CATEGORY 1: LLM FOUNDATIONS
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Attention Is All You Need (2017)
   arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Imagine reading a book but instead of one word at a time, you see the entire
page at once. For every word, you highlight other words most important for
understanding it. The Transformer does this -- looks at ALL words simultaneously
and figures out which are most related using "attention." Before this, AI read
through a tiny keyhole, one word at a time.

  OLD WAY (through a keyhole):
     "The" --> "cat" --> "sat" --> "on" --> "the" --> "mat"
      Each word waits for the one before. Slow!

  TRANSFORMER WAY (whole page at once):
      "The"    "cat"    "sat"    "on"    "the"    "mat"
        |  \   / |  \   / |  \   / |  \   / |  \   / |
        |   \ /  |   \ /  |   \ /  |   \ /  |   \ /  |
        |    X   |    X   |    X   |    X   |    X   |
        |   / \  |   / \  |   / \  |   / \  |   / \  |
        v  /   \ v  /   \ v  /   \ v  /   \ v  /   \ v
      [Every word checks every other word. All at once! Fast!]

Why it matters: This single invention is the engine inside virtually every
modern AI system.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. BERT (2018)
   arxiv.org/abs/1810.04805
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Think of a fill-in-the-blank quiz: "The ___ chased the mouse." BERT reads in
BOTH directions simultaneously -- it sees words before AND after the blank.
Previous AI could only see what came before. Once BERT got good at
fill-in-the-blank, it could be quickly taught specific jobs like spam detection.

  OLD WAY: Only reads left-to-right
      "The"  "cat"  "___"  "the"  "mouse"
       -->    -->    ???    ||||   ||||||    (can't see these!)

  BERT: Reads BOTH directions
       ------>  ------>  ------>  ------>
      "The"    "cat"    "[MASK]"  "the"   "mouse"
       <------  <------  <------  <------
      BERT guesses: "chased"  Correct!

Why it matters: One AI "brain" could learn language deeply, then be quickly
adapted to dozens of jobs.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. GPT-2 (2019)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A child who does nothing but listen to people talk for years, predicting what
word comes next. Nobody teaches grammar or worksheets. Over time, it can write
essays, summarize stories, translate -- all without being taught those skills.
Lesson: make the model bigger + more text = new abilities just appear.

  TRAINING: Just predict the next word, millions of times
      "Once upon a ___"              --> "time"
      "The capital of France is ___" --> "Paris"
      x 1,500,000,000 brain cells
      x millions of web pages

  RESULT: Abilities nobody taught it!
      +------------+  +------------+  +------------+
      | Write      |  | Summarize  |  | Translate  |
      | stories    |  | articles   |  | languages  |
      +------------+  +------------+  +------------+
          All from just predicting the next word!

Why it matters: Scale up a simple recipe and surprising new abilities emerge
on their own.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. GPT-3 (2020)
   arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

175 billion brain cells. Discovery: "in-context learning." Show it a few
examples in the prompt and it figures out new tasks without retraining. Like:
"hello->bonjour, goodbye->au revoir, thank you->?" It says "merci." Anyone
could program AI just by talking to it.

  PROMPT:
  English: "hello"    -> French: "bonjour"    (example 1)
  English: "goodbye"  -> French: "au revoir"  (example 2)
  English: "thank you"-> French: ???

  GPT-3: "merci"   No retraining needed!

Why it matters: Teach AI new tasks just by giving examples in plain English.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Scaling Laws (2020)
   arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Found precise mathematical formulas predicting how well a model performs based
on size, data, and compute. Smooth, predictable curves. Turned AI development
from guesswork into engineering.

  +----------+    +----------+    +----------+
  | MODEL    |    | TRAINING |    | COMPUTE  |
  | SIZE     | +  | DATA     | +  | (GPUs)   |  = Predictable Quality
  +----------+    +----------+    +----------+

Why it matters: Reliable roadmap turning "How big?" from guesswork into
solvable math.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. InstructGPT (2022)
   arxiv.org/abs/2203.02155
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Like dog training. Humans show good examples -> humans rank outputs -> build
scorecard (reward model) -> AI practices using the scorecard. This process
(RLHF) is the secret sauce that turned raw GPT into ChatGPT.

  Step 1: Humans show good examples
  Step 2: Humans rank outputs -> build Reward Model
  Step 3: Model improves using scorecard
       +--------+  generate   +-----------+  score  +----------+
       | GPT-3  | ---------> | REWARD    | ------> | Update   |
       |        | <--------- | MODEL     |         | & repeat |
       +--------+  improve   +-----------+         +----------+
  BEFORE: "Here is a 2000-word essay about dolphins..."
  AFTER:  "Sure! Here are 3 key facts about dolphins."

Why it matters: Cracked the problem of making powerful AI obedient and
helpful -- ancestor of ChatGPT.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. LLaMA (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2302.13971
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Smaller model trained on MORE data beats model 10x its size. LLaMA-13B beat
GPT-3-175B. Like a small kitchen that practices recipes a thousand times
beating a giant one that practices a few times. Open for everyone.

  GPT-3:  175B params x 300B tokens  = "big oven, few recipes"
  LLaMA:   13B params x 1T+ tokens  = "small oven, TONS of practice"
  Result:  LLaMA 13B >= GPT-3 175B on many benchmarks!

Why it matters: Don't need a billion-dollar budget for top-tier AI. Open access.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Llama 2 (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2307.09288
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Improved second edition: 40% more data, longer memory, RLHF chat versions,
published safety testing. Like giving away the car free WITH seatbelts,
airbags, and crash-test report.

Why it matters: Gold standard for releasing powerful open AI responsibly.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. Mixtral 8x7B (2024)
   arxiv.org/abs/2401.04088
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Hospital with 8 specialist doctors. Triage nurse sends each patient to just
2 of 8. Total 47B params, only 13B active at a time. Matches 70B models at
fraction of cost.

  Word comes in -> ROUTER picks 2 of 8 experts
                  /                     \
              [Expert 3]            [Expert 7]
                  \                     /
                   -> Combined answer

  TOTAL: 47B  |  ACTIVE: 13B  |  MATCHES: ~70B models

Why it matters: Smart routing of specialists is cheaper, faster, and just
as good.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. DeepSeek-V2 (2024)
    arxiv.org/abs/2405.04434
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Compressed conversation memory (KV cache) by 90% using shorthand that expands
on demand. Plus 160 tiny experts (vs Mixtral's 8 big ones). Way cheaper to run.

Why it matters: Slashed memory costs by ~93%, making powerful AI affordable
at scale.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. DeepSeek-R1 (2025)
    arxiv.org/abs/2501.12948
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

No textbook, no tutor -- just "right/wrong" feedback. Through pure RL, the
model spontaneously started checking its own work. Matches OpenAI o1.
Open source.

  Traditional:  Human writes steps -> AI copies
  DeepSeek-R1:  AI tries -> Right/Wrong -> repeat millions of times
  EMERGED ON ITS OWN: step-by-step thinking, self-checking, reconsideration

Why it matters: AI can learn to reason and self-correct through practice alone.


================================================================================
                      CATEGORY 2: REASONING & AGENTS
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Chain-of-Thought Prompting (2022)
   arxiv.org/abs/2201.11903
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Show a kid how to solve a math problem by writing out each step, they copy the
pattern. Same with AI -- show it examples with thinking written out, and it
stops skipping steps.

  WITHOUT CoT:  [Question] -------> [Answer] (often wrong!)
  WITH CoT:     [Question]
                    |
                [Step 1: First, I notice...]
                    |
                [Step 2: That means...]
                    |
                [Step 3: So I calculate...]
                    |
                [Answer] (much better!)

Why it matters: Simply asking AI to "show its work" dramatically improves
accuracy.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Self-Consistency (2022)
   arxiv.org/abs/2203.11171
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Ask AI the same question multiple times with different reasoning paths, pick
the answer that comes up most often. Like asking 10 friends independently and
going with the majority.

                    [Same Question]
                   /    |    |    \
                  v     v    v     v
              [Path A][Path B][Path C][Path D]
                 |      |      |       |
                (42)   (42)   (37)    (42)
                  \     |      |      /
                  VOTE: 42 wins! (3/4 agree)

Why it matters: Majority vote catches mistakes a single attempt would miss.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Tree of Thoughts (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2305.10601
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Chess player thinking: explore multiple paths, evaluate each, backtrack from
dead ends. Instead of one chain, AI branches like a tree.

                     [Problem]
                    /    |    \
                [Idea A][Idea B][Idea C]
                /    \     |       |
            [A1]   [A2]  [B1]   [C1]
            Good!   XX   Good!   XX     (XX = dead end)
             |             |
           *BEST*       (backup)

Why it matters: Explore, evaluate, and backtrack lets AI solve creative puzzles.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Scaling Test-Time Compute (2024)
   arxiv.org/abs/2408.03314
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Two ways to improve: (1) bigger brain (expensive), (2) more thinking time on
test day (cheaper). A smaller AI that thinks longer can beat a larger model
that answers quickly.

Why it matters: Better answers by spending compute at question time, not just
training time.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. ReAct (2022)
   arxiv.org/abs/2210.03629
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Detective solving a mystery: think ("victim was at docks"), act (go to docks),
observe (find receipt), think again. AI alternates between reasoning and taking
real actions.

    THOUGHT --> ACTION --> OBSERVE
    "I need     [Search]    "Result:
     to find"   "Einstein    Born 1879"
                 birth"
         ^                      |
         +----------------------+
         v
    THOUGHT --> ACTION --> OBSERVE --> FINAL ANSWER

Why it matters: Combining thinking with real actions grounds AI in facts
instead of guessing.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. Toolformer (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2302.04761
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Student discovers calculators, search engines on their own through trial and
error. AI taught itself which tools help and when -- no rules programmed.

Why it matters: AI figures out tool use on its own.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. Voyager (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2305.16291
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Minecraft player with a recipe book. Learns skills, saves them, builds on them
forever. Like a player who writes down every trick they learn.

Why it matters: AI that builds a growing library of reusable skills -- endless
learning.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Generative Agents (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2304.03442
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

25 AI characters in a virtual town, each with memories. They plan their day,
form relationships, organize events -- all emergently. Like The Sims powered
by ChatGPT.

Why it matters: Give AI memory + reflection + planning = believable society
emerges.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. AutoGen (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2308.08155
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Team building a house: plumber, electrician, carpenter, painter, foreman.
AutoGen lets you build AI teams the same way. Agents pass messages like
coworkers.

Why it matters: Assemble specialized AI agents that collaborate like human
coworkers.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. LLM Autonomous Agents Survey (2023)
    arxiv.org/abs/2308.11432
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Encyclopedia of every AI agent design: brain (LLM), memory (experiences),
hands (tools). The field guide to the whole landscape.

Why it matters: The single best map of the AI agents territory.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. SWE-agent (2024)
    arxiv.org/abs/2405.15793
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Brilliant plumber dropped in a huge building with no map vs. one with a floor
plan and labels. Interface design matters as much as intelligence. Custom
workspace -> AI fixed 12.5% of real GitHub bugs.

  BAD: Raw terminal -> AI lost -> 0% fixed
  GOOD: Custom interface (search, view, edit, test) -> 12.5% fixed
  LESSON: Good tools >= raw brainpower

Why it matters: Workspace design matters as much as how smart the AI is.

                     CATEGORY 3: MULTIMODAL & VISION
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. CLIP (2021)
   arxiv.org/abs/2103.00020
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Child who grew up with 400M picture books. Matches images with text
descriptions. Can recognize things nobody trained it on, just by text.

  [Photo of dog]        "A golden retriever in a park"
       |                          |
   IMAGE ENCODER           TEXT ENCODER
       |                          |
   [vector] <-- MATCH? --> [vector]

Why it matters: Understand images through language -- recognize anything you
can describe.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Flamingo (2022)
   arxiv.org/abs/2204.14198
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Show 3-4 examples of "image + question + answer" and it figures out new
images. Quick learner from few examples.

Why it matters: Learn new visual tasks from just a handful of examples.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. LLaVA (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2304.08485
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Connected "seeing" AI (CLIP) to "talking" AI (LLaMA) with a bridge.
Open-source multimodal assistant.

Why it matters: Build see-and-talk AI by connecting existing parts with a
thin bridge.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. GPT-4V (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2303.08774
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The smartest person you know suddenly gets perfect eyesight. Understands
photos, charts, memes, handwriting. Passed the bar exam.

Why it matters: First AI millions could use to discuss anything they can
photograph.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Segment Anything / SAM (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2304.02643
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Magic scissors for images. Point at any object, it outlines it perfectly.
Trained on 1 billion masks.

Why it matters: Instantly cut out any object from any photo.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. DDPM (2020)
   arxiv.org/abs/2006.11239
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Start with painting, gradually add noise until pure static. Train AI to
reverse: turn static back into image step by step. Foundation of ALL modern
image generators.

  FORWARD:  [Clean] --> [Fuzzy] --> [Very noisy] --> [Pure static]
  REVERSE:  [Static] --> [Vague] --> [Almost clear] --> [Clean!]

Why it matters: The engine inside every major AI image generator.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. Latent Diffusion / Stable Diffusion (2021)
   arxiv.org/abs/2112.10752
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Compress image to tiny version, do creative work there, decompress back. Same
quality, way faster. Made AI art accessible on regular laptops.

Why it matters: AI art generation fast and cheap enough for regular laptops.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. DALL-E 2 (2022)
   arxiv.org/abs/2204.06125
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Type words -> CLIP understands concept -> diffusion paints it. Brought AI art
to mainstream public.

Why it matters: AI-generated art went mainstream.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. Imagen (2022)
   arxiv.org/abs/2205.11487
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Making TEXT understanding bigger matters more than making IMAGE part bigger.
Better listening > better painting.

Why it matters: Key to better AI art is better listening, not better painting.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. Stable Diffusion 3 / MMDiT (2024)
    arxiv.org/abs/2403.03206
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Text and image flow through SAME network, constantly influencing each other.
Finally solved AI being terrible at text in images.

Why it matters: Solved the problem of AI art being terrible at spelling words
in images.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. InternVL (2023)
    arxiv.org/abs/2312.14238
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Scaled vision encoder to 6B params. Great eyes matter as much as great brain.

Why it matters: AI's "eyes" as important as its "brain."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
12. Sora (2024)
    openai.com/index/video-generation-models-as-world-simulators/
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Generates 1-minute videos from text. Understands physics, 3D, camera movement.
"World simulator."

Why it matters: Leap from still images to coherent, physics-aware video.


================================================================================
                CATEGORY 4: RLHF, ALIGNMENT & FINE-TUNING
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Fine-Tuning LMs from Human Preferences (2019)
   arxiv.org/abs/1909.08593
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Like training a dog: try, get feedback, build auto-judge, practice. The
original recipe behind ChatGPT.

Why it matters: Foundational recipe for teaching AI what humans want.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. LoRA (2021)
   arxiv.org/abs/2106.09685
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Freeze the piano, clip on a tiny attachment. 10,000x fewer trainable params,
same performance.

  Traditional: Change ALL billions of settings -> $$$, days
  LoRA:        Freeze + tiny adapters          -> $, hours
  Result:      Both equally good!

Why it matters: Affordable AI customization for anyone.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Prefix-Tuning (2021)
   arxiv.org/abs/2101.00190
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Sticky notes on a textbook. 0.1% of parameters updated, steers the whole
model.

Why it matters: Steer a giant AI with absurdly small changes.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Constitutional AI (2022)
   arxiv.org/abs/2212.08073
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Give AI a list of rules ("constitution"), let it self-check and revise.
Automates safety.

Why it matters: AI learns safety at scale without human reviewing every
response.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Reward Model Overoptimization (2022)
   arxiv.org/abs/2210.10760
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Student games the test: score goes up, knowledge goes down. Same with AI.
Goodhart's Law.

Why it matters: Blindly maximizing scores can make AI worse.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. DPO (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2305.18290
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Old: 3 complex steps. DPO: show "good vs bad" pairs + simple formula. No
reward model, no RL.

  OLD (RLHF): 3 steps, 4+ models, HIGH complexity -> Good
  NEW (DPO):  1 step, 1 model, LOW complexity     -> Equally good!

Why it matters: Preference training accessible to everyone.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. QLoRA (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2305.14314
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Compress model to 4-bit + attach LoRA. Fine-tune 65B on single consumer GPU.

Why it matters: Powerful AI customization on hobbyist hardware.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Representation Engineering (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2310.01405
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Found "volume knobs" inside AI for honesty, morality, power-seeking. Read and
adjust at runtime.

Why it matters: Control AI behavior through internal "personality dials."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. SPIN (2024)
   arxiv.org/abs/2401.01335
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Painter compares work to masterpiece, improves each round. Self-competition
without human feedback.

Why it matters: AI self-improves through self-competition.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. KTO (2024)
    arxiv.org/abs/2402.01306
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Only needs thumbs up/down (not pairs). Based on psychology: losses hurt 2x
more than gains.

Why it matters: Much cheaper data collection for alignment.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. ORPO (2024)
    arxiv.org/abs/2403.07691
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Merge language training and preference training into ONE step. Half the time,
half the compute.

Why it matters: Cuts training pipeline nearly in half.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. REALM (2020)
   arxiv.org/abs/2002.08909
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Student + librarian learn together. Librarian finds pages, student reads them,
both improve.

Why it matters: AI that looks things up while learning = smarter, more factual.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. RAG (2020)
   arxiv.org/abs/2005.11401
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Open-book exam. AI searches textbook first, writes answer using those pages.

Why it matters: Answers backed by sources, reduces "making things up."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Dense Passage Retrieval (2020)
   arxiv.org/abs/2004.04906
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Every passage gets a meaning fingerprint. Find closest match by meaning, not
exact words.

Why it matters: Search by meaning, not keywords -- dramatically more accurate.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Sentence-BERT (2019)
   arxiv.org/abs/1908.10084
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Comparing 10,000 sentences: 65 hours before, 5 seconds after. Permanent

Why it matters: Compare millions of sentences instantly.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Matryoshka Representation Learning (2022)
   arxiv.org/abs/2205.13147
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Russian nesting dolls: first 64 dims are decent, 128 better, 256 great. One
model, many sizes.

Why it matters: One model at different quality/speed levels.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. GPTQ (2022)
   arxiv.org/abs/2210.17323
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Repaint a million-color painting with 8-16 colors using clever math. 175B
model: 350GB -> 80GB.

Why it matters: Massive AI models on ordinary people's computers.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. AWQ (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2306.00978
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Only 1% of weights really matter. Protect those VIPs, compress rest
aggressively. Better than GPTQ.

Why it matters: Smart compression knows what matters most.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Speculative Decoding (2022)
   arxiv.org/abs/2211.17192
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Fast intern drafts words, boss checks all at once. 2-3x faster, mathematically
identical output.

  WITHOUT: Boss writes one word at a time -> 4 seconds
  WITH:    Intern drafts 4 words (0.2s) -> Boss checks all (1s) -> 1.2s!
           Same exact output! 2-3x faster!

Why it matters: 2-3x faster with zero quality loss -- a rare "free lunch."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. Speculative Sampling (2023)
   arxiv.org/abs/2302.01318
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Same idea, discovered independently by DeepMind. Adds rigorous mathematical
PROOF of correctness.

Why it matters: Mathematical guarantee that output is provably identical.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. Switch Transformers (2021)
    arxiv.org/abs/2101.03961
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Hospital with 100 specialists. Receptionist sends each patient to ONE relevant
specialist. 1 trillion params but each word uses ~1/100th.

Why it matters: Enormously knowledgeable models that stay efficient.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. DeepSeek-V2 (2024)
    arxiv.org/abs/2405.04434
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Compressed conversation memory by 93%. Shorthand that expands on demand. 160
fine-grained experts.

Why it matters: Powerful AI affordable to serve to millions.
